Educational Planning Math

Why the Numbers Matter

Part of understanding homebuying is understanding the math. Here are simple planning examples based on a $500,000 Colorado purchase price, so visitors can see how different types of assistance may affect upfront cash needs.

VA Seller Concession
4%
Up to $20,000

On a $500,000 VA purchase, a 4% seller concession may equal up to $20,000 toward allowable closing costs, depending on VA loan rules, lender guidelines and negotiation.

FHA Seller Concession
6%
Up to $30,000

On a $500,000 FHA purchase, a 6% seller concession may equal up to $30,000 toward allowable closing costs, depending on FHA loan rules, lender guidelines and negotiation.

5% Assistance Example
5%
$25,000

A 5% assistance example on a $500,000 purchase equals $25,000 toward down payment or closing costs, depending on program type, lender, eligibility and program rules.

3% Assistance Example
3%
$15,000

A 3% assistance example on a $500,000 purchase equals $15,000. Many Colorado DPA programs use 3% to 4% as a starting assistance range, depending on program and eligibility.

Example only. Actual availability, eligibility, lender rules, loan type, property, program guidelines and final amounts vary. These numbers are for educational planning purposes only.

These are three common structures for homebuyer assistance. A grant is typically not repaid. A forgivable loan may be forgiven after a set period, often with no repayment required if the buyer meets program conditions such as staying in the home for a certain number of years. A deferred second mortgage is repaid later, usually when the home is sold, refinanced or paid off. Understanding which type a program uses matters because it affects long-term cost and planning. Hero HomeReach explains all three in plain English.

Seller credits, also called seller concessions, are amounts the seller agrees to contribute toward the buyer's closing costs as part of the purchase negotiation. On VA loans, seller concessions are typically capped at 4% of the purchase price for certain costs, which may equal up to $20,000 on a $500,000 purchase, depending on VA guidelines, lender rules and negotiation. On FHA loans, seller concessions may go up to 6%, which may equal up to $30,000 on the same purchase price. Example only. Actual amounts depend on loan type, lender, property and program rules.

CHFA, the Colorado Housing and Finance Authority, is Colorado's statewide housing finance resource. CHFA may offer loans and down payment assistance through approved lenders depending on eligibility, income, purchase price and program rules. Some CHFA paths may be worth exploring for veterans and military buyers depending on their loan type and situation. Hero HomeReach explains CHFA programs in plain English so buyers can understand the options before speaking with a CHFA-approved lender.
