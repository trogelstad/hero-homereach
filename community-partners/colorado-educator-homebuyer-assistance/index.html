Educational Planning Math

Why the Numbers Matter

Part of understanding homebuying is understanding the math. Here are simple planning examples based on a $500,000 Colorado purchase price, so visitors can see how different types of assistance may affect upfront cash needs.

3% Assistance Example
3%
$15,000

A 3% assistance example on a $500,000 purchase equals $15,000 toward down payment or closing costs, depending on program type, lender, eligibility and program rules.

5% Assistance Example
5%
$25,000

A 5% assistance example on a $500,000 purchase equals $25,000. Many Colorado DPA programs offer assistance in the 3% to 5% range, depending on program, lender and eligibility.

FHA Seller Concession
6%
Up to $30,000

On a $500,000 FHA purchase, a 6% seller concession may equal up to $30,000 toward allowable closing costs, depending on FHA loan rules, lender guidelines and negotiation.

Conventional Seller Concession
3%
Up to $15,000

On a $500,000 conventional purchase with less than 10% down, seller concessions may be capped at 3%, which could equal up to $15,000 toward allowable closing costs, depending on lender rules and negotiation.

Example only. Actual availability, eligibility, lender rules, loan type, property, program guidelines and final amounts vary. These numbers are for educational planning purposes only.

CHFA, the Colorado Housing and Finance Authority, is Colorado's statewide housing finance resource. CHFA may offer loans and down payment assistance through approved lenders depending on eligibility, income, purchase price and program rules. CHFA programs are not limited to a specific profession, which means teachers and other school staff who meet income and purchase price limits may be able to explore CHFA options. Hero HomeReach explains CHFA programs in plain English so buyers can understand the options before speaking with a CHFA-approved lender.

MetroDPA is a Colorado Front Range down payment assistance program that provides a deferred second mortgage toward down payment and closing costs. The program is not profession-specific, which means eligible Colorado teachers and school staff who meet income limits and credit requirements may be able to explore it. MetroDPA is a 30-year deferred second mortgage at 0% interest, repaid when the home is sold, refinanced or vacated. Program availability, eligibility, income limits and terms depend on individual circumstances, lender and program rules.

Some national programs, such as Teacher Next Door, market specifically to educators. Colorado's most widely used homebuyer assistance programs, including CHFA and MetroDPA, are not profession-exclusive but are income-based, which means many teachers and school staff may qualify depending on their income, credit, loan type and program rules. Some school districts or local jurisdictions may also have employer-assisted housing programs. Hero HomeReach recommends verifying current program availability with a qualified lender or HUD-approved housing counselor.

These are three common structures for homebuyer assistance. A grant is typically not repaid. A forgivable loan may be forgiven after a set period, often with no repayment required if the buyer meets program conditions such as staying in the home for a certain number of years. A deferred second mortgage is repaid later, usually when the home is sold, refinanced or paid off. Understanding which type a program uses matters because it affects long-term cost and planning. Hero HomeReach explains all three in plain English.
