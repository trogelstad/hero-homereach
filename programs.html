State & National

The Foundation: State-Backed Programs

These programs form the backbone of most hero homebuyer packages in Colorado. Wide eligibility, strong amounts and designed to stack with other programs.

✦ CHFA — True Grant
3%
of purchase price that never needs to be repaid

CHFA True Grant

Colorado Housing Finance Authority's flagship grant. Unlike a loan, this money never needs to be repaid. Pairs with CHFA's SmartStep and FirstStep mortgage products. Income limit up to $126,200 for SmartStep.

  • True grant. Zero repayment ever
  • 3% of the purchase price
  • FICO 620+ required
  • Income limit: up to $126,200 (SmartStep)
  • Must use a CHFA-approved lender
  • Available statewide
🏠 SmartStep / Preferred Program
CHFA — Deferred Loan
4%
deferred second mortgage

CHFA Deferred Second Mortgage

A second mortgage that carries no monthly payment. It's deferred until you sell or refinance. Significantly reduces cash needed at closing without adding to your monthly obligations.

  • No monthly payment while in home
  • Repaid only upon sale or refinance
  • 4% of purchase price
  • Works with FHA, VA, conventional loans
  • Available statewide
🏠 FirstStep / FirstGeneration Program
✦ CHFA — FirstGen
$25,000
maximum assistance

CHFA FirstStep / FirstGeneration

The highest-value CHFA product, designed for first-generation homebuyers. If neither you nor your parents have owned a home, you may qualify for up to $25,000. Income limit is generous at $174,440. Most hero households qualify easily.

  • Up to $25,000 maximum benefit
  • First-generation homebuyer requirement
  • FICO 620+ required
  • Income limit: up to $174,440
  • Below-market interest rate on first mortgage
🎓 Common match for educator heroes
✦ Forgiven After 36 Mo.
6%
fully forgiven. Denver Metro / Front Range

MetroDPA

One of the most powerful programs available. MetroDPA provides up to 6% of the purchase price that is completely forgiven after just 36 months of continuous primary occupancy. Stay three years and it's yours free and clear.

  • 100% forgiven after 36 months occupancy
  • Covers Denver Metro and Front Range
  • Up to 6% of purchase price
  • FICO 640+ required
  • Household income up to $210,150
  • Strong option for first responders and nurses
🚒 Popular with first responders
National Program
5%
forgiven after 36 on-time payments

Chenoa Fund

A national safety-net program with unusually flexible eligibility. No income limits and accepts FICO scores as low as 600 making it the go-to option for heroes who don't qualify for income-restricted programs. Forgiveness is triggered by 36 consecutive on-time mortgage payments.

  • No income limits. Open to all heroes
  • Accepts FICO scores as low as 600
  • 5% assistance on FHA loans
  • Forgiven after 36 consecutive on-time payments
  • Available nationwide, including all Colorado
  • Safety net when other programs don't fit
🏥 Great safety net for healthcare workers
Local & Regional

Local Funds — Often Overlooked, Always Valuable

County and regional programs are frequently missed because they're not well publicized. These are some of the highest-value programs available, especially in the Denver Metro and Douglas County areas.

Douglas County
$15,000
flat second mortgage at 3% fixed

Douglas County Housing Partnership

The DCHP traditional program offers a straightforward $15,000 second mortgage at a fixed 3% rate. Predictable, affordable, and stackable with state programs. Note the purchase price cap of $456,000.

  • $15,000 flat second mortgage
  • 3% fixed interest rate
  • Household income at or below 80% AMI (~$124,950)
  • Purchase price cannot exceed $456,000
  • Douglas County residents / buyers
  • Stackable with CHFA programs
✦ Douglas County — Equity Share
$41,000
zero-payment shared equity loan

DCHP Shared Equity Program

The highest single-program amount available in the region. Zero monthly payments — repaid via a share of the home's appreciation when you sell. A powerful tool for heroes buying in Douglas County.

  • Up to $41,000 in assistance
  • Zero monthly payment
  • Repaid via share of home appreciation
  • No out-of-pocket repayment while you live there
📍 Douglas County only
Nonprofit — Statewide
$10–15K
low-interest amortized or deferred loans

Colorado Housing Assistance Corporation (CHAC)

A nonprofit DPA provider offering flexible loan structures for low-to-moderate income buyers across Colorado. Particularly valuable for heroes whose income puts them near the qualifying threshold of other programs.

  • $10,000–$15,000 assistance range
  • Low-interest or deferred options
  • Targets low-to-moderate income buyers
  • Nonprofit — mission-driven, not profit-driven
  • Available statewide
✦ Up to 20% Down
$30,000
0% deferred loan — strict income limits

FirstBank / Impact Development Fund

An exceptional program offering up to 20% of the purchase price (capped at $30,000) as a 0% interest deferred loan. For heroes who meet the income requirements, this can effectively eliminate the down payment entirely.

  • Up to 20% of purchase price
  • Maximum $30,000 in assistance
  • 0% interest — no interest charges ever
  • Deferred — no payment until sale/refi
  • Strict income limits apply
Specialty Hero Grants

Programs Built Specifically for Heroes

These programs exist because of your profession. They're designed to honor your service with real financial assistance, not just a handshake.

✦ True Grant
$8,000
true grant + up to $15K DPA

Teacher Next Door (TND)

Broader than the name suggests, this program covers educators, nurses, first responders, military and government employees. The $8,000 grant uniquely satisfies the mandatory $1,000 minimum borrower contribution required by most DPA programs, plus up to $15,000 in additional down payment assistance.

  • $8,000 true grant that never needs to be repaid
  • Satisfies minimum borrower contribution
  • Additional $15,000 DPA available
  • Eligible: Pre-K–12 teachers, school staff, nurses, first responders, military, government employees
  • No income limits. Stackable with CHFA programs
🎓 Teachers · Nurses · First Responders · Military
HUD Program
50%
off list price · only $100 down payment

HUD Good Neighbor Next Door

A remarkable HUD program offering a 50% discount off the list price on specific HUD-owned foreclosed properties in designated revitalization areas with only a $100 down payment required. The catch: availability is limited, especially in Douglas County.

  • 50% discount off HUD list price
  • Only $100 down payment required
  • Teachers, police, firefighters, EMTs eligible
  • Must commit to living there 3 years
  • Limited inventory — check availability often
  • Rare but extraordinary when available
🚒 First responders + educators
Essential Workers
$15,000
flat grant assistance

NeighborhoodLIFT

A Wells Fargo-partnered program providing up to $15,000 in flat grant assistance specifically for essential workers including first responders, teachers, and military service members. Offered periodically in targeted markets worth monitoring for availability in your area.

  • Up to $15,000 in flat grant assistance
  • Targeted toward essential workers
  • First responders, teachers, military eligible
  • Offered in specific markets periodically
  • Worth monitoring for availability
🎖️ Includes military members

A specialized Colorado program with assistance tailored specifically for firefighters, K-12 teachers, law enforcement, healthcare professionals and military members. Ask us about current availability and amounts in your area.

Post-Closing
0.7%
post-closing rebate of purchase price

Homes for Heroes (HfH)

A post-closing rebate program that replenishes your savings after closing rather than reducing upfront costs. Not traditional DPA — but a meaningful amount that hits your bank account after the transaction completes.

  • 0.7% of purchase price back after closing
  • Replenishes savings — doesn't reduce upfront cash
  • All hero audiences eligible
  • Best used alongside upfront DPA programs
💰 Pairs with upfront DPA
Post-Closing Gift
Varies
closing cost gifts — reimbursed after closing

Everyday Hero Housing Assistance Fund (EHHAF)

Provides closing cost assistance that is gifted and reimbursed after closing. Another post-closing tool that pairs well with upfront programs to maximize total assistance for hero homebuyers.

  • Closing cost assistance — gifted funds
  • Reimbursed after closing completes
  • Pairs with upfront DPA programs
  • Available to hero profession buyers
🏥 All hero audiences

First Mortgage Options

The Right Loan Makes the Stack Work

DPA programs layer on top of a first mortgage. Choosing the right underlying loan type is critical. Some programs only work with specific loan types and your choice affects how much assistance you can access.

🎖️

VA Loan

The gold standard for military members and veterans. Zero down payment required, competitive rates and allows up to 4% in seller concessions. Can often be stacked with Colorado state and local grant programs.

0% down · Military / Veterans only
🏛️

FHA Loan

The most DPA-compatible loan type. Requires 3.5% down (often covered by DPA), allows up to 6% in seller concessions and works with the widest range of assistance programs including Chenoa Fund and CHFA products.

3.5% down · Widest DPA compatibility
🏡

Fannie Mae HomeReady

A conventional option with cancellable PMI meaning once you reach 20% equity the mortgage insurance can be cancelled. Accepts FICO scores of 620 and works well for heroes with moderate income and good credit.

3% down · Cancellable PMI · FICO 620+
🏘️

Freddie Mac Home Possible

Another strong conventional option at 3% down with PMI cancellation. Competitive for heroes who want a conventional loan experience with minimal down payment and the ability to eliminate mortgage insurance over time.

3% down · PMI cancellation option
🌾

USDA Loan

A powerful zero-down option for heroes willing to buy in designated rural areas of Colorado. Geographic restriction is the key limitation but for the right buyer in the right area, this is an exceptional product.

0% down · Rural areas only
⚠️

High-Interest DPA Products

Beware of DPA products like Access Zero that carry interest rates 2+ percentage points above the primary loan rate. They reduce upfront costs but cost significantly more over time. We help you avoid these traps.

Watch out for high-rate DPA products
