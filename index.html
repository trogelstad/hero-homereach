Colorado offers multiple program types that can often be combined. Here's what heroes typically access.

Open the Door With Statewide DPA

CHFA programs reduce or eliminate the barrier of saving a down payment. Some options are structured as grants while others are deferred second mortgages repaid later.

  • Discover assistance that could lower your upfront cash
  • Turn the down payment hurdle into a real strategy
  • Find the CHFA path that could move you closer to home
  • A smart starting point for teachers, first responders, healthcare workers, veterans, and public service buyers

Up to $30,000
in forgivable assistance

Find Money That May Not Have to Be Repaid

There are programs designed to help buyers move forward now, with assistance that may be forgiven over time.

  • Explore programs that could reduce your down payment burden
  • See how forgivable assistance may change your buying power
  • Find options built for buyers who need more than savings alone
  • Different paths can fit different buyer situations. Learn the best path for you!

The Power Move

Understand Your Assistance Paths.

Colorado heroes often have more than one program worth reviewing. Understanding which paths may apply to your situation, and how they interact, is how you protect your money and make a confident decision.

Find My Options
Assistance Paths · $500K FHA Example
Broad CHFA Path
SmartStep Plus may offer a 3% grant or 4% deferred second mortgage $14,475–$19,300
Specialized CHFA Path
FirstGeneration may offer up to $25,000 for eligible first-generation buyers $25,000
metroDPA Path
Up to 6% in eligible Front Range areas $30,000
Chenoa Fund Path
Up to 5% FHA down payment assistance $25,000
Potential Seller Concessions
FHA may allow seller-paid costs up to 6% $30,000
Potential Planning Range $44K–$60K+
These are example paths, not one combined stack. CHFA, metroDPA, and Chenoa are generally best reviewed as alternative DPA paths tied to specific loan and lender rules. Seller concessions may help offset allowable costs, depending on the offer, loan type, actual costs, and lender approval. Final eligibility depends on program rules, lender review, income, credit, property, and available funding.
