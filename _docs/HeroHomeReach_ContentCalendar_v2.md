## Content Strategy: Three Lanes

| Lane | Share | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| **Lane 1: Hero Program Content** | 55% | Convert public service workers into Hero Strategy Session bookings. Deep program education, audience-specific guides, myth-busting. |
| **Lane 2: Colorado Buyer Education** | 20% | Evergreen how-to content that answers buyer questions at every stage. Builds topical authority and trust. |
| **Lane 3: Colorado Market & News** | 25% | Time-sensitive market updates, rate coverage, housing data. SEO traffic driver. Every post must include a "What This Means for Colorado Public Service Workers" bridge section. |

**Tone across all lanes:** Warm, plain-English, Colorado-specific, helpful. No hype. No guarantees. No "free money," "you qualify," "claim," "approved," or "no cash needed."

**Bridge rule (Lane 3 only):** Every market or news post must include a section titled exactly: **"What This Means for Colorado Public Service Workers"** that connects the broader topic back to Hero HomeReach's audience and mission.

**Refresh cadence:**
- Lane 3 housing market posts: Refresh quarterly
- Lane 3 mortgage rate posts: Refresh monthly
- All posts: "Last reviewed [Month Year]" near the top

## Source Standards

All market or data claims must cite one of:
- Colorado Association of Realtors (coloradorealtors.com)
- Zillow Research (zillow.com/research)
- CHFA official site (chfainfo.com)
- Freddie Mac Primary Mortgage Market Survey
- U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics
- Federal Reserve / FRED economic data
- Colorado General Assembly (leg.colorado.gov)
- U.S. Department of Veterans Affairs (va.gov)
- HUD.gov

Avoid: predictions stated as certainty, market opinions without sourcing, specific rate quotes without noting they vary by borrower, lender, and date.
