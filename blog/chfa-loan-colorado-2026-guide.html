CHFA Loan Colorado 2026: The Complete Guide for Hero Homebuyers

Program Guide All Heroes May 2026 16 min read

You serve Colorado. But buying a home here can feel like trying to climb a fourteener with a backpack full of closing costs. That is where a CHFA loan may help.

CHFA is one of Colorado's most important homebuyer resources. But it can also feel confusing at first. Is it a loan? A grant? A second mortgage? A first-time buyer program? The answer is that it can be several of those things, and understanding which one fits your situation matters more than the headline number.

For Colorado teachers, nurses, healthcare workers, firefighters, EMTs, law enforcement officers, military members, veterans, and other public service professionals, CHFA may be one of the first programs worth understanding before house hunting. You can see how it compares to other options on our Colorado down payment assistance programs page.

But the most important detail to understand first is this: the CHFA grant and the CHFA second mortgage are not the same thing. One may not need to be repaid. The other may not require monthly payments now, but can still need repayment later. Let's make that plain-English clear.

Quick Answer: What Is a CHFA Loan in Colorado?

A CHFA loan is a Colorado home financing option offered through approved CHFA participating lenders. Eligible buyers may use CHFA home loan programs with down payment or closing cost assistance.

In 2026, CHFA lists two main down payment assistance options:

  • A CHFA Down Payment Assistance Grant up to the lesser of $25,000 or 3% of the first mortgage loan amount
  • A CHFA Down Payment Assistance Second Mortgage Loan up to the lesser of $25,000 or 4% of the first mortgage loan amount

The grant does not require repayment. The second mortgage does not require monthly payments, but repayment is deferred until certain events happen, such as selling, refinancing, paying off the first mortgage, or no longer living in the home as your primary residence.

What Is CHFA?

CHFA stands for the Colorado Housing and Finance Authority. It is not a regular bank. CHFA works through a statewide network of approved and participating lenders. In simple terms: CHFA is a family of Colorado homebuyer programs offered through approved lenders.

CHFA may help eligible buyers access home purchase loan programs, refinance loan programs, down payment assistance, closing cost assistance, homebuyer education, and special programs for certain buyer situations. For many Colorado buyers, CHFA is not the only option. But it is often one of the most important options to compare. You can review current CHFA program details at the official CHFA down payment assistance page.

Who Should Look at a CHFA Loan?

CHFA is not only for public service professionals. But many Colorado heroes may be strong candidates to explore it, including teachers, school staff, nurses, healthcare workers, firefighters, EMTs, paramedics, police officers, sheriff's deputies, corrections officers, 911 operators, military members, veterans, first-generation homebuyers, first-time homebuyers, buyers with disabilities, and households supporting someone with a disability.

You do not have to be a teacher, nurse, firefighter, or veteran to look at CHFA. But if you are a Colorado hero trying to buy near the community you serve, CHFA deserves a place on your comparison list. See how different hero audiences approach CHFA on our Who We Serve page.

CHFA Is Not Just for First-Time Buyers

This is one of the first myths to clear up. Some CHFA programs are focused on first-time homebuyers. Others may allow qualified veterans or buyers purchasing in targeted areas. Program rules vary by loan type and buyer profile.

Do not assume you are out just because you owned a home before. The better move is to ask which CHFA program fits your situation, whether your veteran status matters, whether the property location matters, and whether you meet income and credit guidelines. CHFA is not a single doorway. It is more like a hallway with several doors. The right lender helps you figure out which one is unlocked.

Do not ask only "Can I get CHFA?" Ask "Which CHFA program fits me best?" Those are very different conversations.

CHFA Down Payment Assistance: Grant vs. Second Mortgage

This is the heart of the whole guide. CHFA offers two main types of down payment assistance: the CHFA Down Payment Assistance Grant and the CHFA Down Payment Assistance Second Mortgage Loan. They sound similar. They are not.

CHFA Down Payment Assistance Grant

The CHFA grant may provide up to the lesser of $25,000 or 3% of the first mortgage loan amount. CHFA states that this grant does not require repayment. That makes it the cleaner form of assistance for many buyers. In plain English: the CHFA grant can help reduce your upfront cost without creating a future repayment obligation. But the grant amount may be lower than the second mortgage option because it is capped at 3%.

CHFA Down Payment Assistance Second Mortgage Loan

The CHFA second mortgage loan may provide up to the lesser of $25,000 or 4% of the first mortgage loan amount. This option may provide more assistance than the grant. But it is not a grant. The second mortgage does not require monthly payments, but repayment is deferred until certain trigger events happen, including selling the home, refinancing, paying off the first mortgage, or stopping use of the home as your primary residence.

In plain English: the CHFA second mortgage can lower your cash needed now, but it may still need to be repaid later. That does not make it bad. It just means you need to understand the future math before you choose it. Our guide to the 3 types of Colorado down payment assistance explains this in full detail.

Important: CHFA says borrowers may obtain either the CHFA Down Payment Assistance Grant or the CHFA Down Payment Assistance Second Mortgage Loan with a CHFA first mortgage, but generally not both together. You are choosing one structure, not stacking both.

Simple Example: Buying a $500,000 Home

Say a Colorado buyer is purchasing a $500,000 home and their first mortgage amount is about $485,000.

CHFA Grant Example: 3% of $485,000 = $14,550. Because $14,550 is less than the $25,000 cap, that buyer might see a CHFA grant amount up to about $14,550, depending on eligibility and final loan structure. No repayment required.

CHFA Second Mortgage Example: 4% of $485,000 = $19,400. Because $19,400 is less than the $25,000 cap, that buyer might see a CHFA second mortgage amount up to about $19,400. More help upfront, but repayment may be triggered later.

The second mortgage may provide more upfront help. But the grant may be cleaner because it does not require repayment. So the question is not only "Which one gives me more money?" The better question is: Which one gives me the best long-term outcome?

CHFA Loan Requirements in 2026

1. You Must Use a CHFA Participating Lender

You do not get a CHFA loan directly from CHFA. You work through a participating lender who helps determine which CHFA program may fit, which loan type you qualify for, whether you meet income and credit requirements, whether the property is eligible, and which assistance option may be available. This is one reason it helps to ask about CHFA early. If your lender does not offer CHFA, you may not get a real comparison.

2. Income Limits May Apply

CHFA programs generally have income limits. The exact limit can depend on county, household size, program type, loan type, targeted area status, and buyer profile. You want your lender to check the current CHFA income limit for your exact situation, not a blog post.

3. Credit Score Rules May Apply

Many CHFA program highlights reference a minimum decision credit score of 620 or higher for scored borrowers, though details vary by program and loan type. Your full approval can also depend on debt, income, assets, loan type, property, and lender underwriting.

4. Homebuyer Education Is Required

CHFA says all CHFA borrowers must individually and independently complete a CHFA-approved homebuyer education class before their mortgage loan closing date. Each borrower must receive their own certificate of completion. For first-time buyers, this can be genuinely useful before you are deep in contracts, inspection deadlines, and closing numbers.

5. The Home Must Be Your Primary Residence

CHFA assistance is designed for homeownership, not investment property. The home generally needs to be your primary residence. This also matters for the second mortgage. If the home stops being your primary residence, that can be one of the trigger events for repayment.

6. Loan Type Matters

CHFA programs may work with FHA, VA, USDA-RD, and conventional options such as Fannie Mae HFA Preferred or Freddie Mac HFA Advantage. A veteran may compare CHFA with VA financing. A first-time buyer may compare FHA and conventional options. This is why CHFA should be compared, not blindly chosen.

CHFA Programs Worth Knowing

You do not need to memorize every CHFA product name. But it helps to know the major lanes.

CHFA FirstStep is often discussed for first-time homebuyers, qualified veterans, or buyers purchasing in targeted areas.

CHFA FirstGeneration is designed for first-generation homebuyer situations. If your parents or guardians did not own a home, or if you meet CHFA's first-generation criteria, this may be worth exploring.

CHFA SmartStep is another program option depending on borrower profile, loan type, and program rules.

CHFA HomeAccess may help eligible buyers with a permanent disability, or households where the borrower is the custodial parent or legal guardian of someone with a permanent disability.

The practical takeaway: do not walk in asking only "Can I get CHFA?" Ask "Which CHFA program fits me best?"

CHFA for Colorado Heroes

CHFA is not hero-exclusive. But it may be very relevant for Colorado heroes.

Teachers and school staff might compare CHFA with Teacher Next Door benefits, seller-paid closing costs, and local DPA options. For teachers trying to buy in the community where they work, CHFA can be one of the statewide tools to compare. See the full breakdown in our Colorado teacher homebuyer guide.

Nurses and healthcare workers might compare CHFA with healthcare worker savings programs, metroDPA, Chenoa Fund, and lender credits. Common CHFA Mistakes to Avoid

Thinking CHFA is only for first-time buyers. Some CHFA options focus on first-time buyers, but veterans or buyers in targeted areas may have different paths. Do not rule yourself out too early.

Thinking every CHFA option is a grant. CHFA has a grant option and a second mortgage option. One does not require repayment. One may require repayment later. They are not the same.

Thinking "no monthly payment" means "free." The CHFA second mortgage may not require monthly payments. But deferred does not mean forgiven. Repayment may be triggered when you sell, refinance, pay off the first mortgage, or stop using the home as your primary residence.

Waiting until after house hunting. CHFA can affect your loan type, lender choice, cash-to-close, education requirements, and timeline. Check early.

Using a lender who does not offer CHFA. Ask directly: "Are you a CHFA participating lender?" If the answer is no, you may never see the option clearly.

Ignoring income limits. Income limits can vary by program and location. Do not assume. Verify with your lender.

Assuming every program stacks. Some programs can work together. Others cannot. The stacking review needs to happen before you are under pressure to make an offer.

How to Start With CHFA

Step 1: Check your basic fit. Start with household income, approximate credit score, whether you are a first-time buyer or veteran, what county you are buying in, how much you have saved, and whether you plan to live in the home as your primary residence.

Step 2: Talk with a CHFA participating lender. A participating lender can help you compare the CHFA grant, CHFA second mortgage, FHA, VA, USDA, conventional options, seller credits, and other assistance programs.

Step 3: Complete CHFA homebuyer education. If CHFA is the right fit, complete the required CHFA-approved homebuyer education before closing. Do not leave this until the last second.

Step 4: Compare grant vs. second mortgage. Ask how much help you get with each, whether the second mortgage needs repayment later, what happens if you refinance or sell, and which option gives you the better long-term outcome.

Step 5: Compare CHFA against other programs. CHFA may be excellent. But compare it with metroDPA, Chenoa Fund, VA loan options, hero-specific programs, seller credits, lender credits, and local city or county assistance. The best plan is rarely one-size-fits-all.

FAQ: CHFA Loan Colorado 2026

What is a CHFA loan in Colorado?

A CHFA loan is a Colorado home financing option offered through CHFA participating lenders. Eligible buyers may use CHFA loan programs with grants or second mortgage loans for down payment and closing cost assistance.

Is CHFA only for first-time homebuyers?

Not always. Some CHFA programs are focused on first-time buyers, but qualified veterans or buyers purchasing in targeted areas may also have options. Program rules vary.

How much down payment assistance does CHFA offer in 2026?

CHFA lists a grant option up to the lesser of $25,000 or 3% of the first mortgage loan amount, and a second mortgage option up to the lesser of $25,000 or 4% of the first mortgage loan amount.

Does the CHFA grant have to be repaid?

CHFA states that its down payment assistance grant does not require repayment.

Does the CHFA second mortgage have to be repaid?

The CHFA second mortgage does not require monthly payments, but repayment is deferred until certain events happen, including sale, refinance, first mortgage payoff, or the home no longer being your primary residence.

Can I use the CHFA grant and second mortgage together?

Generally no. CHFA says borrowers may obtain either the CHFA Down Payment Assistance Grant or the CHFA Down Payment Assistance Second Mortgage Loan with a CHFA first mortgage, but not both together.

What credit score do I need for CHFA?

Many CHFA program highlights reference a minimum decision credit score of 620 or higher for scored borrowers. Requirements vary by program, loan type, and lender underwriting.

Can teachers, nurses, firefighters, EMTs, and veterans use CHFA?

Yes, eligible public service professionals may be able to use CHFA if they meet the program requirements. CHFA is not limited only to heroes, but many Colorado heroes may want to compare it.

Can CHFA be used with VA loans?

Some CHFA program options may work with VA financing, depending on the program and lender guidelines. Veterans should compare CHFA, VA loan benefits, seller credits, and other assistance options.

Is CHFA better than metroDPA?

Not always. CHFA is statewide and has grant and second mortgage options. metroDPA serves many Front Range communities and has its own assistance structure. The better fit depends on where you are buying, your income, credit, loan type, and long-term plans.
