VA Loan Down Payment Assistance in Colorado: What Veterans Should Know

Program Guide Military & Veterans May 2026 12 min read
Share

Buying a home in Colorado can feel a little ridiculous right now. You served your country. You did the hard things. And now a starter home can feel like it requires a treasure map, a spreadsheet, and possibly a small miracle.

Here is the good news: if you are a veteran, active-duty service member, or eligible surviving spouse, your VA home loan benefit may already be one of the strongest homebuying tools available. But here is the part many people miss. Your VA loan benefit may not be the only help available. Depending on your situation, you may also be able to use Colorado down payment assistance, closing cost help, or local homebuyer programs to reduce the cash needed to buy a home. That is where Colorado homebuyer help for veterans and heroes comes in.

Quick Answer: Can Veterans Use Down Payment Assistance With a VA Loan?

Yes, in some cases, veterans may be able to use down payment assistance with a VA loan in Colorado.

A VA loan often allows eligible buyers to purchase with no VA-required down payment, no private mortgage insurance, and limited closing costs. You can review those VA home loan benefits directly through the Veterans Benefits Administration.

But "zero down" does not always mean "zero cash needed." You may still need money for closing costs, prepaid taxes and insurance, earnest money, inspection and appraisal costs, moving expenses, and cash reserves.

Colorado programs like CHFA and metroDPA may help with some of these upfront costs, depending on your income, credit, lender, loan type, location, and the specific property. You can explore more options on our Colorado down payment assistance programs for heroes.

What Makes the VA Loan So Powerful?

The VA loan is not a "special coupon." It is a hard-earned benefit. For eligible veterans, active-duty service members, and certain surviving spouses, a VA-backed purchase loan can make homeownership more realistic by reducing some of the biggest barriers buyers face.

According to the VA, VA-backed purchase loans may offer no down payment as long as the sales price does not exceed the appraised value, better terms and interest rates than many private loans, limited closing costs, no private mortgage insurance, and the ability to use the benefit more than once. You can read the official VA explanation on the VA-backed purchase loan page.

That last point matters. Some veterans assume the VA loan is only for first-time buyers. Not true. The VA home loan benefit is a lifetime benefit and can be used multiple times, as long as you qualify and meet program rules.

Your VA benefit may open the door. Assistance programs may help you walk through it with a little more breathing room.

If VA Loans Can Be Zero Down, Why Does Assistance Still Matter?

This is where a lot of homebuyers get surprised. A VA loan may remove the need for a traditional down payment. That is a huge deal. But the down payment is only one piece of buying a home.

You may still need money for closing costs, taxes, insurance, inspections, moving costs, and other real-world expenses that show up right when your bank account is trying to act calm. The VA also explains that borrowers may still need to pay a VA funding fee and other closing costs on VA-backed or VA direct loans. You can see the official breakdown on the VA's funding fee and closing costs page.

That is why VA loan down payment assistance in Colorado can still matter. Even if your down payment is $0, assistance may help reduce other costs connected to getting into the home. Think of it this way: the VA loan may help with the mountain climb. Down payment assistance may help carry the backpack. And in Colorado, where housing costs can make even confident buyers blink twice, every bit of help matters.

Colorado Programs Veterans Should Know About

Colorado has several homebuyer assistance options that may be relevant for veterans and military families. The right fit depends on where you are buying, your income, your credit, your lender, and the type of mortgage being used.

CHFA Down Payment Assistance

CHFA, the Colorado Housing and Finance Authority, is one of the most important statewide homebuyer resources in Colorado. CHFA offers eligible borrowers a Down Payment Assistance Grant and a Second Mortgage Loan. The DPA grant may offer up to 3% of the first mortgage loan amount and does not require repayment. The second mortgage loan may offer up to 4% of the first mortgage loan amount, with repayment deferred until certain events such as sale, refinance, first mortgage payoff, or the home no longer being the borrower's primary residence. You can review CHFA's official down payment assistance page.

Important: A grant may not need to be repaid. A deferred second mortgage usually does not require monthly payments right away, but it may become due later. Always ask which type of assistance you are receiving before signing anything, and always ask whether it is compatible with a VA loan through your specific lender.

metroDPA

metroDPA is another major Colorado program, especially for buyers in the Denver metro and Front Range areas. The official metroDPA program offers 30-year fixed-rate mortgage loans, including VA loans, along with borrower assistance for down payment, closing costs, and prepaids. You can review the official metroDPA borrower program page. Denver's metroDPA information notes that eligible borrowers may qualify with income up to $216,000 and a credit score above 640, with some cases allowing 620. You can also review Denver's official metroDPA program information.

Colorado DOLA Down Payment Assistance

The Colorado Department of Local Affairs has a Down Payment Assistance Program designed to help qualified low- to moderate-income families afford homeownership. Colorado DOLA says this program is available to local jurisdictions and housing partners, not directly to individual homebuyers, meaning you would typically access help through approved local partners. You can review the official Colorado DOLA Down Payment Assistance page.

Can You Stack a VA Loan With Down Payment Assistance?

Sometimes, yes. But this is where we want to be careful. Stacking homebuyer benefits is not like stacking grocery coupons. It is more like building a safe bridge. Every piece has to fit.

A VA loan may be compatible with certain assistance programs, but the final answer depends on the assistance program rules, the lender's guidelines, your income, your credit score, your debt-to-income ratio, the home's location, whether the home will be your primary residence, whether the program requires a homebuyer education course, and whether the assistance is a grant, forgivable loan, or deferred second mortgage.

This is why Hero HomeReach focuses on helping you ask better questions early. Because once you are under contract, the clock starts running. Grant vs. Forgivable Loan vs. Deferred Second Mortgage

Not all "assistance" works the same way. Before using any program, make sure you understand what kind of help you are receiving.

A grant is usually the cleanest form of help. If you meet the rules, it may not need to be repaid. CHFA's DPA grant is one example of assistance that CHFA says does not require repayment, subject to program rules.

A forgivable loan may be forgiven after you meet certain requirements, such as living in the home for a certain number of years. If you sell too soon or refinance too early, you may have to repay some or all of the assistance.

A deferred second mortgage typically does not require monthly payments right away, but it is not the same as free money. CHFA explains that repayment of its second mortgage assistance may be deferred until certain events occur, including first mortgage payoff, sale, refinance, or the home no longer being your primary residence. This can still be very helpful. You just deserve to know exactly how it works.

Common Mistakes Veterans Make When Buying in Colorado

Assuming zero down means zero cash needed. A VA loan may eliminate the required down payment, but closing costs and prepaid expenses can still apply.

Talking to only one lender. Not every lender is equally experienced with VA loans and Colorado assistance programs. A lender may understand VA loans but not participate in certain DPA programs. That matters.

Waiting too long to ask about assistance. Ask before you shop. Some programs have rules that need to be handled early in the process.

Not asking what kind of assistance it is. Grant? Forgivable loan? Deferred second mortgage? Those are three very different structures. Always ask before signing.

Shopping before knowing the full cash-to-close number. The monthly payment matters. But so does the amount needed at closing. A good plan looks at both.
