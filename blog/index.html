Common Questions

FAQ

The questions we hear most from Colorado heroes — answered directly, in plain English.

Do I have to be a first-time homebuyer to qualify?
+
Not always. Many programs — including CHFA's SmartStep, MetroDPA, and Teacher Next Door — are available to repeat buyers as well as first-time buyers. Some programs, like CHFA FirstGeneration, are specifically for first-time buyers. We'll identify which programs apply to your situation during your free consultation.
What credit score do I need to qualify?
+
It depends on the program. MetroDPA requires a 640+ FICO. CHFA programs require 620+. Chenoa Fund accepts scores as low as 600 — making it a strong safety-net option for heroes whose credit isn't perfect. Most programs have more flexibility than people expect.
I have significant student loan debt. Can I still qualify?
+
Often yes. Student loans affect your debt-to-income ratio (DTI), but many Colorado programs work with lenders who understand hero income profiles. Teachers and healthcare workers with student loans qualify regularly. The key is finding the right program and loan structure — which is exactly what we help with.
Is my income too high to qualify?
+
Probably not. Income limits are higher than most heroes expect. MetroDPA allows up to $210,150 household income. CHFA FirstGen allows up to $174,440. Teacher Next Door and Chenoa Fund have no income limits at all. Most Colorado hero households qualify for at least one — and often multiple — programs.
I just moved to Colorado. Can I still access these programs?
+
Yes. Most programs have no Colorado residency requirement beyond intending to use the property as your primary residence. Travel nurses, recently transferred military members, and new hires qualify regularly. Where you work or plan to live matters more than how long you've been in the state.
What's the difference between a grant and a forgivable loan?
+
A grant is free money — you never repay it under any circumstances. A forgivable loan starts as a loan but is forgiven (wiped away) after a qualifying period — typically 3 years of continuous occupancy or 36 on-time mortgage payments. Both result in money you don't have to pay back if you meet the conditions.
What is MetroDPA and who can use it?
+
MetroDPA is a regional program covering the Denver Metro and Front Range area. It provides up to 6% of the purchase price — completely forgiven after 36 months of continuous primary occupancy. Requirements: 640+ FICO, household income under $210,150. It's one of the most powerful programs available and works for all four hero audiences.
Does Teacher Next Door only cover teachers?
+
No — despite the name, Teacher Next Door covers a wide range of professions. Eligible buyers include Pre-K–12 teachers, school staff, nurses, first responders, military members, and government employees. It provides an $8,000 true grant plus up to $15,000 in additional DPA, with no income limits on FHA products.
Do these programs work in all Colorado counties?
+
Program availability varies by county. CHFA and Chenoa Fund are statewide. MetroDPA covers the Denver Metro and Front Range. DCHP programs are specific to Douglas County. Some local municipality programs are city-specific. During your free consultation, we'll map the programs available for your exact location.
Can I use a VA loan and still access Colorado programs?
+
Yes — and this is one of the biggest missed opportunities for military members. Many Colorado state and local programs can be stacked on top of VA loan benefits, potentially adding $10,000–$25,000 in additional assistance beyond your VA benefit. This is exactly the kind of stack we identify in your free consultation.
What happens during the free consultation?
+
The call is 30 minutes. We'll discuss your profession, income range, location, credit situation, and homebuying goals. From there, we'll map every program you qualify for, explain how they work together, calculate your maximum combined assistance, and give you a clear next-steps plan. No pressure, no sales pitch.
What should I have ready for the consultation?
+
Just yourself and a general sense of your situation — your profession, a rough income range, whether you've bought before, and the area of Colorado you're looking in. You don't need documents, exact numbers, or anything formal. We'll figure out the details together.
How long does the homebuying process take after the consultation?
+
It varies, but most heroes have a clear picture of their options within a week of their consultation. From there, the timeline depends on finding the right home — but the program eligibility and stack are in place. Many buyers close within 60–90 days of starting the process.
Is my information kept private?
+
Absolutely. Your information is never sold, shared with advertisers, or forwarded to lenders without your explicit consent. Hero HomeReach™ is an education and guidance service — not a lead generation company. What you share stays with us.
Can I really combine multiple programs?
+
Yes — and this is the core strategy of what we do. Stacking means combining non-conflicting grants, forgivable loans, and seller contributions so you cover all down payment and closing costs without using your own savings. A Colorado teacher on a $400,000 FHA purchase can access up to $67,000 in combined assistance.
What are seller concessions and how do they factor in?
+
Seller concessions are credits negotiated as part of your purchase offer — the seller agrees to contribute toward your closing costs. FHA loans allow up to 6% in seller concessions (up to $24,000 on a $400K home). This isn't a program — it's a negotiation tool. When combined with grants and DPA, it's what makes a $0 out-of-pocket closing possible.
How do I know which programs can be stacked together?
+
That's exactly what we figure out in your free consultation. Program stackability depends on your loan type, the specific programs involved, your income, and your location. Not all programs stack with all others — but many do. We map the legal combinations and show you your maximum possible benefit.
What is the maximum amount a hero can receive in Colorado?
+
On a $400,000 FHA purchase, the maximum stack for a qualifying teacher is: Teacher Next Door Grant ($8,000) + Teacher Next Door DPA ($15,000) + Chenoa Fund 5% ($20,000) + Seller Concessions 6% ($24,000) = $67,000 in total combined assistance — resulting in $0 out-of-pocket at closing.
Is the consultation really free? What's the catch?
+
It's genuinely free — no catch, no hidden fees, no obligation. Hero HomeReach™ is a homeownership education and guidance service. The consultation costs you nothing and requires nothing in return. If you find the information useful and decide to move forward, we can connect you with the right resources. If not, you've lost 30 minutes and gained a clearer picture of your options.
Is Hero HomeReach™ a lender or mortgage company?
+
No. Hero HomeReach™ is an education and guidance service — not a lender, mortgage broker, or real estate agent. We don't originate loans or collect commissions on transactions. Our job is to educate you on what's available and help you make an informed decision. You choose if, when, and how to proceed.
Will I be pressured to use a specific lender or agent?
+
Never. Our consultations are pressure-free and lender-neutral. We can provide referrals to CHFA-approved lenders if you request them, but we never push or incentivize any specific provider. Who you work with is entirely your decision.
Myth Busters

The Beliefs That Keep Heroes From Buying

These are the six most common misconceptions we hear from Colorado heroes — and the truth that changes everything.

The Myth
"I need 20% down to buy a home."
The Truth
Most hero programs require 3–3.5% down — and DPA grants and forgivable loans can cover that entirely. A $0 out-of-pocket closing is achievable with the right stack.
The Myth
"These programs are only for low-income buyers."
The Truth
MetroDPA allows household income up to $210,150. CHFA FirstGen up to $174,440. Teacher Next Door and Chenoa Fund have no income limits. Most hero households qualify easily.
The Myth
"My VA loan is the only benefit I can get."
The Truth
Colorado state and local programs can often stack directly on top of VA benefits — adding $10,000–$25,000 in additional assistance that most veterans never know exists.
The Myth
"My credit score isn't good enough."
The Truth
Chenoa Fund accepts FICO scores as low as 600. CHFA programs start at 620. MetroDPA at 640. There's a program for almost every credit profile — and we'll find the right fit.
The Myth
"Student loans make it impossible for me to qualify."
The Truth
Student loans affect your DTI — but teachers and nurses with six-figure student debt qualify regularly. The key is matching your situation to the right program and loan structure.
The Myth
"These grants have a catch — I'll have to pay it back."
The Truth
True grants are never repaid — period. Forgivable loans are forgiven after 3 years or 36 on-time payments. There are no hidden clawbacks if you meet the program terms.
